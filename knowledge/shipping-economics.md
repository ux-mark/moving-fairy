# Shipping Economics — Skill Module

## Cost Benchmarks

Sea freight for household moves is priced per cubic metre (CBM), not per kilogram. Standard industry benchmark: €150–250 per CBM for US → Ireland sea freight (door-to-door).

| Route | Cost per CBM (LCL sea freight) | Transit time |
|-------|--------------------------------|--------------|
| US → IE | ~$150–250 / CBM | 6–8 weeks |
| US → AU | ~$180–300 / CBM | 8–12 weeks |
| IE → AU | ~€200–350 / CBM | 6–10 weeks |

- LCL (less than container load) is typical for partial household shipments.
- FCL (full container, 20ft) is economical at ~15 CBM+ and runs ~$3,000–6,000 US→IE.
- Get quotes from at least three international freight or removal companies.
- These are rough benchmarks — actual quotes vary by volume (CBM), port pairs, and fuel surcharge periods.

### Standard Box CBM Reference

| Box size | Approx. CBM | Cost estimate (US → IE) |
|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| XS       | 0.04 CBM    | ~€6–10                 |
| S        | 0.07 CBM    | ~€10–18                |
| M        | 0.15 CBM    | ~€22–38                |
| L        | 0.25 CBM    | ~€37–63                |

Single items: calculate as `(L cm × W cm × H cm) / 1,000,000 = CBM`.

## The 2× Rule

**If the like-for-like replacement cost of an item at the arrival country ≤ 2× its shipping cost, replace on arrival.**

Example:
- Item occupies 0.15 CBM (standard M box). Shipping cost US → IE: 0.15 CBM × $200/CBM = $30.
- 2× shipping cost = $60.
- If the item can be replaced in Ireland for ≤$60, do not ship it. Replace on arrival.
- If replacement in Ireland costs >$60, ship it.

Apply this rule per item. Factor in:
- Like-for-like replacement (same brand/spec, not a downgrade).
- Availability in the arrival country — some US-spec items are not available abroad.

## Two-Leg Shipping Strategy

Applies when `onward_country` is set in the user profile. Guidance depends on `onward_timeline`.

### onward_timeline = "1-2yr"

Be highly selective about what you ship to the intermediate country. You will be moving again very soon.

- **Ship to IE**: Irreplaceable items only — heirlooms, archival material, original artwork, sentimental items. Also items with very high AU replacement cost.
- **Do not ship to IE**: Anything bulky, heavy furniture, large appliances. The IE → AU leg will cost more than it's worth for these.
- **Consider**: Shipping definitively AU-bound items (heirlooms, archival material) direct to AU storage now, bypassing the IE leg entirely.
- **In IE**: Live light. Buy second-hand only. Nothing bulky.

### onward_timeline = "3-5yr"

Moderate selectivity. Ship meaningful belongings to IE now; reassess the AU leg later.

- **Ship to IE**: Apply the standard 2× rule. Also ship anything with high AU replacement cost.
- **In IE**: Buy second-hand furniture and homewares only. Do not buy large appliances beyond what you need — if you are shipping appliances from the US via transformer, you do not need to rebuy.
- **Reassess**: At the 2.5–3 year mark, begin planning the IE → AU shipping. Get quotes. Cull IE belongings before shipping — a second edit reduces cost.
- **Consider**: Items that are definitively AU-bound can ship direct to AU storage now.

### onward_timeline = "5yr+" or "undecided"

Ship to IE; treat AU as a separate future decision with no time pressure.

- Apply the standard 2× rule for shipping to IE.
- Do not optimise for AU yet — the landscape will change.
- When the AU move becomes real, run through this module again with AU as arrival country.

### Exception: Direct-to-AU Shipping

For items that are definitively and permanently Australia-bound (heirlooms, archival documents, original artwork, items too bulky to practically move twice), consider shipping direct to AU storage from the US — bypassing the IE leg.

- US → AU sea freight is not dramatically more expensive than US → IE.
- Eliminates the IE → AU leg cost entirely.
- Requires the user or a trusted contact in AU to receive and store the goods.

## What to Acquire in Ireland

Follow these rules to avoid paying to move things twice:

| Category | Guidance |
|----------|---------|
| Furniture | Second-hand only (DoneDeal, Facebook Marketplace) or IKEA. Nothing expensive. |
| White goods | Rent furnished if possible. If you must buy, buy cheap second-hand. |
| Bedding / homewares | Buy cheap; easy to discard or charity-shop before AU move. |
| Clothing | Buy freely — ships cheaply or can be culled. |
| Books | Buy freely — cheap to ship, easy to donate. |
| Electronics | Buy freely — 230V in IE and AU, so these move without issue. |
| Specialty appliances | Do not rebuy what you are shipping from the US via transformer. |

## Timing Checklist

### Now (US — Before Departure)

- [ ] Sell large appliances, vehicles, and furniture with poor shipping economics.
- [ ] Get at least 3 shipping quotes.
- [ ] Inventory items being shipped — document for insurance.
- [ ] Clean outdoor gear, bicycles, and golf clubs if bound for AU.

### In Ireland

- [ ] Buy second-hand or disposable furniture only.
- [ ] Do not acquire anything bulky you will regret moving again.

### Reassessment Point (Before AU Move)

- [ ] Get IE → AU shipping quotes.
- [ ] Apply the 2× rule again against current AU replacement costs.
- [ ] Edit belongings — cull anything not worth two legs of international freight.
