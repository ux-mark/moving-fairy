# Australia Onward / Arrival — Knowledge Module

Applies when `onward_country = AU` or `arrival_country = AU`.

---

## Electrical

- **Voltage**: 230V / 50Hz
- **Plug type**: Type I (two or three flat angled blades)
- Australia and Ireland share the same voltage and frequency — items bought or used in Ireland will work in Australia with only a plug adapter change.
- US-only (120V) items still require a step-down transformer in Australia exactly as in Ireland. The Dynastar DS-5500 (or equivalent) works the same way in AU as in IE.

---

## Plug Adapter Note — DS-5500 and US Transformers

If the user owns a US step-down transformer (e.g., the Dynastar DS-5500), it has a Type A/B US output socket. In Australia, power the transformer via a Type I → Type B adapter on the input side. The transformer output still runs the US-voltage appliance as normal.

## Import Restrictions and Biosecurity (affects shipability)

Australia's biosecurity regime (administered by DAFF — the Department of Agriculture, Fisheries and Forestry) is the single biggest factor for items being shipped from Ireland or the US. These rules affect the verdict directly — items that are prohibited cannot be shipped; items that are high-risk should be flagged or reconsidered.

### Prohibited — do not ship

- **Firearms**: Require an import permit from the Australian Federal Police plus a state licence. For most household moves: **SELL before departure**.
- **Seeds and fresh or dried plant material**: Prohibited without permits. **DISCARD before shipping**.
- **Certain self-defence items** (pepper spray, tasers, extendable batons): Prohibited in most Australian states. **SELL or DISCARD**.
- **Ivory and CITES-listed species products**: Prohibited. Cannot ship.
- **Controlled substances**: Prohibited.

### High biosecurity risk — SHIP with flag (or SELL depending on value)

| Item | Risk | Verdict impact |
|------|------|----------------|
| Timber furniture (untreated) | Must have ISPM 15 heat-treatment certificate. Without it: treated at owner's cost or destroyed at the border. | SHIP ⚠️ — confirm ISPM 15 with shipping company |
| Organic materials (kapok pillows, feather items, dried herbs) | Often seized by Australian Border Force | SELL in Ireland — not worth the risk |
| Outdoor/camping gear, bicycles, hiking boots | Soil contamination risk — must be thoroughly cleaned before packing | SHIP ⚠️ — clean thoroughly before packing |
| Golf clubs | Same soil contamination risk | SHIP ⚠️ — clean thoroughly |
| Wicker or untreated timber items | High seizure risk | SELL — not worth shipping |
| Seeds or dried nuts | Prohibited | DISCARD |
| Fresh or dried fruit | Prohibited | DISCARD / consume before travel |

### Must declare on arrival (not a shipping concern — for user awareness)

Many items must be declared on arrival in Australia even if they are not prohibited — food, plant material, animal products. This is a customs declaration matter; see the Customs feature for guidance [link placeholder].

## Cost of Living — Replace-at-Destination Notes

Australia is generally expensive for appliances and consumer goods:

- KitchenAid and US-brand stand mixers: comparable to or higher than Irish prices; shipping from US or IE is worth it for high-spec units.
- Professional power tools: DeWalt, Milwaukee available in AU but at higher prices than US; shipping can be cost-effective.
- Basic homewares, kitchenware, and bedding: widely available at reasonable prices — IKEA has Australian stores (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, Canberra, Adelaide).
- Second-hand markets are strong: Gumtree and Facebook Marketplace for furniture and appliances in major cities.

## Two-Leg Strategy Considerations

For users moving US → IE → AU:

- Items bought or shipped to Ireland in 230V format will work in Australia without any conversion — just a Type I plug adapter.
- The IE → AU leg costs approximately €15–25/kg (LCL sea freight, 6–10 weeks transit). Factor this into decisions about what to accumulate in Ireland.
- Items definitively bound for Australia (heirlooms, archival material, large irreplaceable items) may be worth shipping direct from the US to Australian storage, bypassing the IE leg entirely. US → AU sea freight is not dramatically more expensive than US → IE.
- Consult `knowledge/shipping-economics.md` for the full two-leg strategy by onward timeline.
