# Ireland Arrival / Intermediate — Knowledge Module

Applies when `arrival_country = IE` or `onward_country = IE`.

---

## Electrical

- **Voltage**: 230V / 50Hz
- **Plug type**: Type G (three rectangular blades)
- US-bought dual-voltage items (100–240V) need only a Type G plug adapter.
- US-only (120V) items need a step-down transformer or should be replaced.

---

## Import Restrictions (affects shipability)

These rules determine whether an item can be shipped to Ireland. They directly affect the verdict — do not treat this as customs paperwork guidance.

| Item | Status | Verdict impact |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Firearms and ammunition | Prohibited without advance Irish firearms licence | SELL before departure — do not ship |
| Controlled substances | Prohibited | Cannot ship — carry with prescription documentation only |
| Meat and dairy from non-EU countries | Prohibited | DISCARD or consume before travel |
| Plants and plant material | Phytosanitary inspection required | SHIP with caution — may be inspected or seized |
| Pets | Permitted with EU Pet Travel compliance — microchip, rabies vaccination, EU health certificate | Not a packing item — flag as a separate logistics task requiring 4–6 months lead time |

For duty-free allowances, TOR relief applications, and customs forms, see the Customs feature [link placeholder].

## Cost of Living — Replace-at-Destination Notes

Ireland is generally more expensive than the US for appliances and electronics. Key considerations for the 2× rule:

- Stand mixers, food processors, and kitchen appliances: 40–60% more expensive than US retail.
- Professional power tools: significantly more expensive; US prices are hard to beat.
- Basic kitchenware, bedding, and homewares: comparable or only slightly higher — not worth shipping.
- IKEA is present in Ireland (Dublin and Belfast) — furniture basics are available at similar prices to the US.
- Second-hand markets are active: DoneDeal and Facebook Marketplace for furniture and appliances.

## Ireland as an Intermediate Country

If `onward_country` is set (e.g., AU), apply these rules for what to acquire in Ireland:

**Buy second-hand or cheap only — you will move again:**
- Furniture: IKEA, Facebook Marketplace, DoneDeal. Nothing bulky or expensive.
- Bedding, kitchenware, homewares: Buy new if cheap; second-hand is fine.
- Avoid: Sofas, large wardrobes, dining tables, white goods — the economics of shipping these to AU do not work.

**Buy freely:**
- Clothing, books, small electronics — these ship cheaply or can be culled before Australia.
- Electronics are 230V and will work in Australia unchanged (AU is also 230V / 50Hz; just swap to a Type I adapter).

**Do not rebuy appliances you are shipping from the US** — if you are bringing a stand mixer or power tools via transformer, you do not need to replace them in Ireland.
