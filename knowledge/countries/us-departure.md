# US Departure — Knowledge Module

Applies when `departure_country = US`.

---

## Electrical

- **Voltage**: 120V / 60Hz
- **Plug type**: Type A (two flat blades) / Type B (two flat + round ground)
- Ireland and Australia run 230V / 50Hz — all US-only appliances are incompatible without a transformer, and the 60→50Hz frequency shift affects motors and heating elements regardless.

---

## Export Restrictions

These restrictions directly affect the verdict — items that cannot be shipped must be sold, surrendered, or carried under specific conditions before departure.

- **Firearms and ammunition**: Cannot be shipped internationally. Must be sold or transferred to a licensed dealer before departure. Verdict: **SELL** (or surrender).
- **Controlled substances and certain medications**: Cannot be shipped internationally. Carry personal prescription medications in hand luggage with a doctor's letter; check arrival country rules for controlled substances before travelling.
- **Certain electronics and technology (EAR/ITAR)**: High-end military or dual-use technology may require US export licences. This is rare for household moves but flag it if the user mentions items such as night-vision equipment, advanced radio hardware, or specialist defence-sector tools.

---

## Items to Sell Before Leaving the US

These have strong US resale markets and poor viability abroad:

| Item | Reason to Sell |
|------|---------------|
| Large appliances (washer, dryer, fridge, dishwasher) | US drum/cavity dimensions don't fit Irish/AU kitchens; compressor motors degrade at 50Hz |
| Hair dryers and flat irons | 50Hz degrades heating elements; cheap to replace abroad |
| Drip coffee makers (Keurig, Breville, etc.) | Cheap to replace; Irish hard water damages them anyway |
| US-spec vacuum cleaners | 120V only; not worth shipping; cheaper to replace |
| Gas appliances | Ireland/AU use different fittings; not shippable |
| Large furniture | Economics almost never work across a transatlantic move |

---

## Items Worth Shipping from the US

These cost significantly more in Ireland or Australia, making shipping cost-effective:

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| KitchenAid / stand mixer (with transformer) | IE/AU price 40–60% higher than US; transformer makes it viable |
| Professional power tools (DeWalt, Milwaukee) | Significantly cheaper in US; check transformer compatibility |
| Specialty cookware (All-Clad, cast iron, carbon steel) | Comparable prices but heavy; only if already owned and valued |
| Books, clothing, linens | Cheap to ship by weight if volume is there |
| Sentimental items, heirlooms, artwork | Ship regardless of cost |
| Specialty outdoor/sports gear | Can be expensive to replace like-for-like |

---

## Items Cheap or Easy to Replace in IE/AU (Not Worth Shipping)

- Cheap kitchenware, flatware, glasses
- IKEA-type furniture
- Basic bedding and towels
- Cleaning supplies, toiletries
- Low-end electronics and accessories
- Supermarket staples (ship nothing perishable)

---

## Where to Sell in the US

- **Facebook Marketplace** — best for furniture, appliances, and general household goods
- **Craigslist** — still effective for appliances and bulky items in most metro areas
- **OfferUp** — good for electronics and smaller items
- **eBay** — best for collectibles, specialty gear, and items with a national buyer pool
- **Local buy-nothing groups** — for items you want gone quickly at no return
- **Used appliance dealers** — will take large appliances for a fraction of value but with zero effort

## Shipping from the US

### Sea Freight Benchmarks (US → IE)

| Route | Cost per kg | Transit time |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| US → IE (sea, LCL) | ~$10–20/kg | 6–8 weeks |
| US → AU (sea, LCL) | ~$12–22/kg | 8–12 weeks |

- LCL (less than container load) is typical for household partial-loads.
- FCL (full container) makes sense only for large whole-house shipments.
- Get quotes from at least three international movers (e.g., Allied, Crown, Seven Seas).
